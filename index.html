01 — About

About Me

I am a Senior Researcher at the University of Eastern Finland (UEF) Law School, where I am a member of the Center for Climate Change, Energy and Environmental Law (CCEEL) and the Global Climate Change Law research group. I hold a PhD from Uppsala University, where I studied the multi-level governance of climate change at the Department of Government.

My research investigates climate governance across multiple levels of governance, employing both qualitative and quantitative methods. I have previously worked at the Wuppertal Institute for Climate, Energy and Environment, and at the think tank Praxis.

My peer-reviewed work has appeared in Global Environmental Politics, World Development, and International Environmental Agreements: Politics, Law and Economics, among other journals.

02 — Research

Research

My research examines how climate change is governed across multiple levels — from international agreements and national policies to local politics and elections. I use both qualitative and quantitative methods to understand what drives climate ambition and how political and economic forces shape environmental outcomes.

01

International Climate Governance

I study the determinants of countries' climate commitments under the Paris Agreement, including what drives NDC enhancement and how international cooperation on fossil fuel supply restriction can affect environmental and economic outcomes.

02

Climate Finance & Policy

My work investigates how climate finance is allocated through bureaucratic and political processes, and how social policy instruments such as welfare state structures can shape public support for carbon taxation.

03

Energy Politics & Elections

I examine how energy-related grievances — such as electricity price shocks — translate into political behaviour and electoral outcomes, linking environmental economics to democratic politics.
